Campus Life

school chapel

Was built in 1988

The school chaplain provides support to individual students and the school community as a whole through pastoral care. In this role the chaplain helps foster a sense of community and Christian values throughout the school. In addition to playing an important role in wellbeing structures and activities the chaplain oversees the chapel and leads regular liturgies and worship services. The chaplain provides a presence with whom students and their families can talk and is available to encourage and support students experiencing emotional and spiritual concerns.

Sports and Aquatic Center

Was built in 2019

The opening of our Sports & Aquatic Centre in 2019 was the realisation of a dream for our community – a dream that started in 1983. With an eight lane 25 metre pool, a learn to swim pool, dance and movement studio and a fitness studio, we have a comprehensive contemporary facility. The Sports & Aquatic Centre is the flagship of our impressive sports precinct in the school which includes netball courts, a full-sized soccer field, two large football ovals, junior ovals, tennis courts, gymnasium and sports annexe.

News about Westbourne

  • Premier’s VCE Awards 2020

    Congratulations to Luke Tarola, Yonglian Cai and Divine Emezie who have been recognised with a Premier’s VCE Award for their outstanding academic results in 2020. Luke Tarola received a Premier’s VCE Award for being a Top All-Round VCE High Achiever, which is given to students who achieved outstanding results in five or more of their subjects. Yonglian Cai received a Premier’s VCE Award for being a Top Three International Student and Divine Emezie was recognised for English. The annual Premier’s VCE Awards recognise the top-performing students in the state. This year, 297 students were recognised with awards for their outstanding results. The entire Westbourne Grammar School community is proud of Luke Tarola, Yonglian Cai, Divine Emezie and would like to congratulate them on being the recipients of such a prestigious award. To read more about the Premier’s VCE Awards, visit: www.education.vic.gov.au/vceawards.
